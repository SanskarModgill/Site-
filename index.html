Explore the Universe

🌌

Milky Way Galaxy

Our home galaxy spans over 100,000 light-years and contains between 100–400 billion stars — including our very own Sun.

🪐

Saturn's Rings

Saturn's iconic ring system stretches up to 282,000 km from the planet, made mostly of ice particles and rocky debris.

⭐

Stellar Nurseries

Nebulae are the birthplaces of stars — vast clouds of gas and dust where gravity causes matter to collapse and ignite.

🌑

Black Holes

So dense that not even light can escape, black holes warp spacetime itself. Supermassive ones lurk at the heart of most galaxies.

🌠

Meteor Showers

When Earth passes through comet debris trails, we're treated to spectacular showers like the Perseids and Leonids.

☀️

Our Sun

A medium-sized star 109 times wider than Earth, the Sun contains 99.86% of the Solar System's total mass.

Space Exploration Milestones

1957

Sputnik 1 — First Satellite

The Soviet Union launched the world's first artificial satellite, marking the dawn of the Space Age.

1969

Apollo 11 — Moon Landing

Neil Armstrong and Buzz Aldrin became the first humans to walk on the Moon on July 20, 1969.

1990

Hubble Space Telescope

Launched into low Earth orbit, Hubble has captured some of the deepest views of the universe ever seen.

2021

James Webb Space Telescope

The most advanced space observatory ever built, designed to observe the universe's first galaxies.

Future

Crewed Mars Mission

Multiple space agencies and private companies are actively working toward landing humans on Mars.
